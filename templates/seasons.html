Deep Dive - Seasonal Impact

Analyze seasonal impact on mortality. Mortality rates higher in winter vs summer? What are the top 5 causes of mortality in different age groups.

.

Overall Seasonal Impact
Contarary to the common belief we dint find any major seasonal impact. Overall higer number of deaths were recorded in winter, however it was on 11.25% compared to summer.
Examining the Top 5 Causes of Deaths in Winter
Its evident that many causes of death have a clear seasonal pattern. Circulatory and respiratory diseases, which comprise about half of all deaths, have a peak in winter and lowest mortality during summer.

Examining the Top 5 Causes of Death of People Under the Age of 15
Over 70% of deaths in this age group attributed to causes that can be prevented with proper education and precautions.
Examining the Top 5 Causes of Death of People Aged 15 - 35
Over 80% of deaths in this age group attributed to issues related to drug abuse, suicides or accidents.
Examining the Top 5 Causes of Death of People Aged 35 - 54
Drug related deaths contributed to highest number of deaths in this age group, followed by heart attacks and respiratory diseases towards the end of the age group.
Examining the Top 5 Causes of Death of People Aged 55 - 85
Respiratory diseases were leading cause of death in this age group followed by heart attacks and diabetes.
