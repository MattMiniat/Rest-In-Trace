General Description

The nervous system is incredibly complex and controls crucial functions necessary to perform daily activities. The nervous system controls the 5 senses plus movement, coordination, and the ability to think and reason. Nervous system disorders can be caused by a variety of reasons including but not limited to: vascular disorders (blood supply), injuries, infections, and tumors. Symptoms of nervous system disorders are impaired speech, movement, cognitive ability, vision, and sense of feeling as well as tremors and seizures.

Prominent Diseases
  • Alzheimer's and other degenerative diseases (64.6% of deaths)
  • Parkinson's and other extrapyramidal disorders (16.1% of deaths)
  • Huntington's and other systemic atrophies (5.1% of deaths)
