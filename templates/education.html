Deep Dive - Education Level

Here we will explore how educational level affect death rates across multiple other demographics.

Examining the Breakdown of Deaths by Education Level and Age Group
We can already see that the elderly make up the highest percentages in our analysis, but is most notable in “8th grade or less” with 44.91%.
Examining the Breakdown of Deaths by Education Level, Age Group, and Gender
We split the data between genders. We can start to piece together an inverse relationship with education, age, and gender. For younger age groups, male deaths take up the majority, whereas for older age groups, usually 75 and older, female deaths become the majority. However, the higher the level of education, the less this trend becomes apparent as male deaths become prevalent in Doctorate and Masters level of education.
Examining the Breakdown of Deaths by Education Level, Gender, and Marital Status
In this observation of between Gender and Education, there are no discernible trends. However, the significant difference between men and women in the “Doctorate” level of education stands out. Next we’ll try marital status and education. We can see that almost 20% of the “Never married, single” category is “8th grade or less” as compared to the other marital statuses. Let’s try to put them together. Already we can see some pretty notable differences, especially with Married Males and Widowed Females. The only education that stands out, however, is the Doctorate category, which remains predominately male. As it stands, education doesn’t appear to have as significant an impact on causes of death as other factors like age do.
Examining the Breakdown of Deaths by Education Level and Race
First we analyze the percentage of deaths per race, per education level. We can already see some big differences, especially with the bachelor’s degree level of education. Focusing on only Bachelor’s Degrees, we look into which Cause of Death Categories are most prevalent for each race. We can see that Asian and Pacific Islander is the only race to have neoplasms(Cancer/Tumors) as it’s leading cause of death. Diving in even further, we’re shown that trachea, bronchus, and lung neoplasms are the leading cause of death, making up ~20% of neoplastic deaths for Asian or Pacific Islanders.
